Subroutine to generate values ' \( Dm \)' and its first ‘ \( Dmp \)’ and second ' \( Dmpp \)' derivatives with respect to \( R \) of the chosen form of the damping function, for \( m = 1 \) to \( MMAX \).

Upon Input

\( r \) is the radial distance in Angsroms.

\( RHOab \) is the 'universal' scaling coefficient used for systems other than \( H_2 \):

\[ RHOab = 2\frac{RHOa*RHOb}{RHOa+RHOb} \]

where \( RHOa = \left(\frac{I_p^A}{I_p^H}\right)^{0.66} \), \( I_p^A \) is the ionization potential of atom A, and \( I_p^H \) is the ionization potential of atomic hydrogen.

\( NCMM \) is the number of inverse-power terms to be considered.

\( MMLR \) are the powers of the \( NCMM \) inverse-power terms.

\( IDF \) requires damping to be defined such that:

\[ \frac{Dm(r)}{r^m} \rightarrow r^{\frac{IDF}{2}} \]

\( IDSTT \) specifies damping function type:

If IDSTT > 0, use Douketis et al. form.

If IDSTT \( \leq \) 0, use Tang-Toennies form.

\( KDER \):

If KDER > 0, the first derivative is also calculated.

If KDER > 1 the second derivative is also calculated.

Upon Output

\( DM(m) \) - The value of the damping function for the long range term ( \( m = 1, NCMM \)):

\[ \frac{C_{MMLR(m)}}{r^{MMLR(m)}} \]

\( DMP(m) \) - The first derivative of the damping function \( DM(m) \)

\( DMPP(m) \) - The second derivative of the damping function \( DM(m) \)
